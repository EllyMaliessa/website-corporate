About the Gallery

Get to know the space dedicated to honouring Malaysia's legendary entertainer.

Gallery P. Ramlee is a cultural heritage venue located in Kuala Lumpur. Established to celebrate and preserve the legacy of Tan Sri P. Ramlee — one of Malaysia’s most iconic and influential figures in music and film — the gallery brings his remarkable story to life through immersive displays.

From vintage movie posters to handwritten lyrics and personal awards, every artifact on display offers visitors a glimpse into Malaysia’s entertainment golden age.

Objectives

  • Preserve & display P. Ramlee’s personal collections
  • Educate younger generations on local performing-arts history
  • Serve as a resource hub for research & inspiration

Milestones

1991 Gallery officially opened to the public.
2005 Interactive audio stations introduced.
2017 Mini cinema & archive corner added.
2024 Virtual-tour experience launched.

Fun Facts

  • 500+ authentic artifacts are housed here.
  • The gallery hosts free weekend film screenings.
  • Entry is completely free for all visitors.
